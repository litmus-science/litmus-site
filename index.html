The Problem

Science has an access problem

The barriers to generating hypotheses have collapsed—AI can suggest thousands of experiments. But the barriers to testing those hypotheses remain as high as ever.

Traditional CROs

Minimum engagements of $10K-50K+. Weeks of quoting before work begins. Not designed for one-off hypothesis testing.

Too expensive

Cloud Labs

Built for programmers, not scientists. Require learning domain-specific languages like SLL or Autoprotocol to encode experiments. Subscriptions start at $24K+/month.

Too technical

Academic Collaborations

Months of relationship-building. Often demand co-authorship. Depend on finding aligned interests.

Too slow
